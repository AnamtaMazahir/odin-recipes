Butter Chicken Recipe

Description

Butter Chicken, or Murgh Makhani, is one of the most popular Indian dishes worldwide. It features tender pieces of marinated chicken simmered in a rich, creamy, and mildly spiced tomato-based gravy. The sauce's velvety texture and complex flavors make it an unforgettable culinary experience.

This recipe simplifies the traditional method while keeping the authentic, beloved taste. It's perfect for serving with basmati rice or warm naan bread to soak up every last drop of the delicious gravy.

Ingredients

For the Chicken Marinade:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (or to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

For the Gravy:

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 (15 ounce) can tomato puree
  • 1/4 cup raw cashews, soaked in hot water for 20 minutes
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon crushed dried fenugreek leaves (Kasoori Methi)

Steps

  1. In a bowl, combine all ingredients for the chicken marinade. Add the chicken pieces, mix well, cover, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour (or up to 8 hours).
  2. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat with a little oil or ghee. Cook the marinated chicken in batches until golden brown and cooked through. Set aside.
  3. To make the gravy, melt butter in the same pan. Add the chopped onion and saut√© until golden. Add the ginger-garlic paste and cook for another minute.
  4. Stir in the tomato puree, garam masala, red chili powder, and sugar. Cook for 10-12 minutes, until the sauce thickens and darkens.
  5. Drain the soaked cashews and blend them with a little water to make a smooth paste. Add this cashew paste to the gravy and cook for 5 more minutes.
  6. Let the gravy cool slightly, then transfer it to a blender and blend until completely smooth.
  7. Return the smooth gravy to the pan. Add the cooked chicken and bring to a simmer.
  8. Stir in the heavy cream and the crushed fenugreek leaves (Kasoori Methi). Simmer for 2 more minutes, but do not boil.
  9. Serve hot with your choice of rice or naan bread.
